Building Basic Sentences

Statement

Noun [N] or Pronoun [P]+Verb [V] + Adjective [Adj] or Adverb [Adv] or Noun [N]

Ma chambre [N] est [V] grande [Adj] = My bedroom is big.

Mon frère [N] aime [V] le fromage [N] = My brother likes cheese.

Elle [P] travaille [V] trop [Adv] = She works too much.

Negative Statement

ne (+verbe) pas” is the typical form to make a negative sentence. The verb located in between “ne” and “pas” is conjugated. When the verb starts with a vowel, “ne” becomes “**n’**”

Question

  • “quand” [ken]= when
  • “qui” [kee] = who
  • “quoi” [kwa] = what
  • “où” [oo] = where
  • “pourquoi” [poorkwa] = why
  • “combien” [konbee-un] = how many/much

Formal: Où habites-tu? [MI+verb+subject]

Informal: Où est-ce que tu habites? [MI+est-ce que+subject+verb]

Familiar: Tu habits Où? [subject+verb+MI]

Articles

Article Male Female Plural
Define Le La Les
Indefinite Un Une Des
Partitifs Du De La —

À+le = Au, À + les = Aux

De+le = Du, De+les = Des

une, une, des changes to de when used in negative sentences
