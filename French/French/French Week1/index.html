Verbs - Irregular Present

être (to be)

one of the most common French verbs used to describe states, feelings, location, identity, origin, and much more.

TALKING… FRENCH ENGLISH
About yourself je suis “I am”
To your interlocutor tu es “you are”
About a man il est “he is”
About a woman elle est “she is”
About you and someone else nous sommes “we are”
About a person formally or a group of people vous êtes “you are”
About men or men and women ils sont “they are”
About women elles sont “they are”

Avoir (to have)

One of the most common French verbs used to describe possession, age, physical and emotional states, and more.

TALKING… FRENCH “ENGLISH”
About yourself j’ai “I have”
To your interlocutor tu as “you have”
About a man il a “he has”
About a woman elle a “she has”
About you and someone else nous avons “we have”
About a person formally or a group of people vous avez “you have”
About men or men and women ils ont “they have”
About women elles ont “they have”

Faire (to do / to make)

One of the most versatile French verbs used to describe a wide range of actions and activities.

TALKING… FRENCH “ENGLISH”
About yourself je fais “I do” / “I make”
To your interlocutor tu fais “you do” / “you make”
About a man il fait “he does” / “he makes”
About a woman elle fait “she does” / “she makes”
About you and someone else nous faisons “we do” / “we make”
About a person formally or a group of people vous faites “you do” / “you make”
About men or men and women ils font “they do” / “they make”
About women elles font “they do” / “they make”

Aller (to go)

A fundamental French verb used to express movement and actions related to going to various places.

TALKING… FRENCH “ENGLISH”
About yourself je vais “I go”
To your interlocutor tu vas “you go”
About a man il va “he goes”
About a woman elle va “she goes”
About you and someone else nous allons “we go”
About a person formally or a group of people vous allez “you go”
About men or men and women ils vont “they go”
About women elles vont “they go”

Adjectives

Most French adjectives are placed after the noun they describe. However, most adjectives expressing Beauty, Age, Number, Goodness and Size (BANGS adjectives) are placed before the noun they describe.In French adjectives must agree in number and gender with the noun they describe.

Add a “e” to adjectives that describe a feminine noun, unless the adjective already ends with a silent “e”
Add a “s” to adjectives that describe a plural noun, unless the adjective already ends in “s”.

Prepeption

French English Example of Use
à to, at, in Nous arriverons à Paris à huit heures. We will arrive in Paris at 8:00.
avant before J’arriverai avant vous. I will arrive before you.
avec with Je vais à Paris avec mon ami. I am going to Paris with my friend.
dans in Elle est dans la salle de séjour. She is in the living room.
de from, of, about Je viens de Chicago. I am coming from Chicago.
en in, on, to Je suis allé en Italie en 2016. I went to Italy in 2016.
pour for Pierre m’a donné un cadeau pour mon anniversaire. Pierre gave me a gift for my birthday.
