System of linear equations

Linear equations An equation in the unknowns $x, y,z,…$ is called linear if both sides of the equation are a sum of (constant) multiples of $x, y,z,…,$ plus an optional constant. For instance, $3x + 4y = 2z$ and $−x − z = 100$. The point $P$ is said to be the initial point of the vector, and the point $Q$ is the terminal point.
